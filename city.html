Step into eternal dusk…

Generations have lived and died beneath the starless skies, leaving nothing but a whisper behind. We find our heroes living in a world like our own, yet different in so many ways. No one quite knows when or how it happened, but everyone knows what to call it: the Cataclysm. It is what plucked the sun from the sky and turned the seas an inky black. It is what brought the dead back to haunt us and what gave some the ability to see beyond the veil. Now the denizens of this world live in perpetual dusk, with no end in sight.

Set in a bustling port city on the verge of industrialization and reminiscent of our own world’s Victorian era London, Amsterdam, or Venice, the gap between wealth and starvation only grows wider with each passing day. It’s crowded with row-houses, twisting streets, and criss-crossed with hundreds of little waterways and bridges. Each with their own story to tell if you know how to listen. Dehumanizing factories and corrupt bureaucrats rule over the poor but there is no shortage of opportunities for those who know where to look, and aren’t afraid to get their hands dirty. Temptation waits around every corner, and only those who can see the unseen have a chance to survive. Perhaps that could just be you...

Welcome to Blades in the Dark, a tabletop game developed by John Harper and Evil Hat Productions. This tale follows the (mis)adventures of an up and coming crew of thieves as they struggles to survive under the ever-present boot that is Doskvol. If you’ve liked such shows as Peaky Blinders or dove into the book series Six of Crows, Blades in the Dark might just be for you. For more information, check out the Blades in the Dark website.

Our crew is yet unnamed, a fledgling band of spies, thieves, and outcasts eking out an existence in Doskvol’s famed Nightmarket. Each has made a devil’s bargain with the city to survive, and though the gifts it has bestowed upon these rogues the price is high for some. That has not deterred them however, and they intend to ply their trade to the highest bidder as a Shadow crew—wading into the mercurial waters of Doskvol’s underworld. Amongst the crew are the wiry saboteur Aldo Banfield, the brooding nobleman Crowl Welker, the ethereal Cross Clermont, and the cut-throat thief Brace Bookman. Each carries their own obligations and baggage, but for better or worse they have set their sites on a piece of the Doskvol pie. If they can work together, they might not just survive but thrive in the shadows of the city.
